Research Projects

Scoliosis Back Brace

During the fall semester of 2024, I collaborated with an exceptional interdisciplinary team of engineering students. Under the expert guidance of Dr. Erkan Kaplanoglu, a distinguished professor at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga (UTC) whose research specializes in biomechatronic systems, we embarked on a challenging yet rewarding project. Our primary objective was to design and develop an innovative Scoliosis Back Brace to treat idiopathic pediatric scoliosis. Our design ethos was rooted in two key goals: reducing the time required to manufacture each brace and creating a product that would adapt to the child's growth, thereby minimizing the need for multiple braces throughout their developmental years. This adaptability was especially crucial, as it addressed frequent brace replacements due to the rapid growth spurts during puberty. Through rigorous research, detailed planning, and collaborative effort, our team explored various materials, design methodologies, and manufacturing techniques. We aimed to integrate advanced biomechatronic principles to enhance the back brace's functionality and comfort. By the end of the semester, our prototype demonstrated promising results, setting the stage for further refinement and potential real-world application. My team had the opportunity to present our research at the 2025 American Society for Engineering Education. At the end of the conference, we were rewarded the ASEE-SE Award for Best Overall Student Poster.

Sensor Devevlopment and Implementation Pipeline – Clarkson University REU

Electric Skateboard (Spring 25)

I had the opportunity to participate in an IEEE competition hosted by the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, where I collaborated with a fellow sophomore majoring in electrical engineering. Together, we designed and built an electric skateboard that seamlessly integrated electrical and mechanical components to optimize performance, efficiency, and control. Our project focused on developing a display layout for real-time diagnostics, refining motor efficiency, and implementing a reliable braking system. Through extensive testing and iteration, we engineered a lightweight yet durable board that met IEEE standards—and our hard work paid off, as our team won first place in the competition. This experience not only strengthened my technical skills in circuit design and joining processes but also fostered teamwork and problem-solving in a real-world engineering challenge.
